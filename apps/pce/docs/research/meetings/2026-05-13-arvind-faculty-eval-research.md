## Current evaluation workflow (Explorance Blue)

### Notification sequence (3 emails from central admin)
1. Window opening notification + modification options
2. Window closing reminder + response rate visible + student reminder capability  
3. Results available notification + portal login link

### Standard timing (2-week window)
- Opens ~1 week before course ends
- Closes before final exam (prevents grade-anxiety influence on feedback)
- Faculty can modify dates within admin-set boundaries

### Portal access
- Multi-factor authentication (Duo text codes, 60-second window)
- Reports available for 12 months (download encouraged — portal reliability concerns)
- No username/password sharing (security layers)

## Response rate challenges

- Common: 8 responses from 60 enrolled students
- Previous paper-based = better: 10-min in-class completion
- Some schools withhold final grades until evaluation completion
- Extended windows don't improve participation vs. dedicated class time
- **Design implication:** Build in-class distribution mode or grade-linking feature (future)

## Three primary uses of evaluation reports

1. **Annual performance review** — pre-filled aggregate scores from dean's office
   - Question 11 example: "How would you rate this faculty member overall?"
   - Automatic averaging across all courses
   - Faculty must explain below-average ratings
2. **Promotion dossier** — requires 5–10 year history
   - Self-compiled portfolio when seeking promotion
3. **Course improvement** — qualitative student comments → action planning
   - Anonymous feedback most valuable
   - Faculty note planned changes for next offering

## Rating scale problems (design implication)

- Transitioning from 5-point to 4-point scale
- 5-point: students defaulted to 3 (middle)
- 4-point: scores converge around 3.2 (minimal differentiation)
- Small differences (3.2 vs 3.25) determine top 20% vs 40% faculty rankings
- **Design implication:** Support configurable Likert scale (3, 4, 5, 7, 10 points) — controlled in settings, not per-survey

## Annual summary gaps (current product pain)

- Dean's office provides aggregate only (school-wide, dept, individual averages)
- No faculty ability to contextualize or compare across their own courses
- No multi-year trend tracking in current portal
- Misleading averages when teaching diverse course types (5-student PhD vs 85-student MBA)
- **Design implication:** Weighted average option (by enrollment size), course-by-course breakdown

## Key design opportunities Arvind identified

### Action plan capture system
- Faculty most engaged immediately after receiving feedback
- 6-month gap before teaching same course → disconnect from feedback
- **Feature idea:** AI-generated action suggestions based on qualitative comments + supervisor review + automated reminder when teaching same course again

### Advanced analytics dashboard
- Multi-year trend tracking across faculty career
- Course-by-course performance comparison within faculty portfolio
- Weighted averaging options (enrollment weight)
- Granular vs. aggregate view toggle

## Awards and peer review (for later phases)

- Golden Apple Award = separate student voting survey (not standard eval)
- Dean's office committee awards: subjective, not purely eval-based
- Peer review: classroom visit assessments + committee annual ranking
- **Implication:** Separate survey module for award voting (not same flow as course evaluation)
