## User Flows

- **Post-exam analytics:** Course coordinator opens completed assessment → sees score distribution overview → drills into per-question analysis (PBI, discrimination) → sees content area / objective / Bloom's coverage breakdown → identifies weak areas
  > "I would put that analysis as level one analysis. It's cumulative. Got it. And then I will get into per question analysis."

- **Live monitoring:** Course coordinator opens assessment during exam → sees flagged questions per student (by question order) → acknowledges or dismisses flags → gets live count of flagged questions

---

## Design Decisions

- **Post-exam analytics ordering:** Overall summary (score distribution) first, then per-question drill-down. Not the other way.
  > "What is useful to flip? Is it better to give them a direct understanding of each question first? [...] I think we can merge it together. It will work out."

- **Three coverage dimensions for question analysis:** Content area, course objectives, Bloom's taxonomy. All three should be visualized.
  > "You may want to just show a graph of these questions. Group those questions based on content area, objectives, Bloom's taxonomy."

- **Content area frequency ≠ percentages:** Show which content areas were covered and how many questions, not percentage bars.
  > "You're trying to show the frequency with which that content area was covered in this assessment. Percentages are not making sense."

- **Accommodations in Phase 1:** Extra time + larger font size are the two most common. Third (separate room) cannot be handled by the product. Speech-to-text and text-to-speech are lower priority.
  > "Extra time, higher font size, and need to sit in a different room are the three most common. If we can cater to those, it's a win for our launch. So if you want to build more advanced things, you can based on bandwidth. But these are the two things we can do."

- **Accommodation visibility for coordinator:** Course coordinator should see which students in their class have accommodations, and what those accommodations are, so they can plan.
  > "Having an accommodation view in our exam tool is a plus. Every course coordinator gets to see that in your class for this course, you have five students that have been permitted accommodations."

- **Student performance in EM:** Show competency/content-area strengths and weaknesses based on EM exam data. NOT overall GPA or academic standing — that belongs in student profile/360 dashboard.
  > "We can show that this student was part of five courses and 10 exams. And across all of them, this is how the student has performed. That we can give. But exam scores are just exam scores — if they curve them later, that happens in LMS."

---

## Review & Approval Workflows

- Post-exam review is about ANALYTICS and question quality, not a workflow approval gate
- PBI (Point Biserial Index) and discrimination coefficient are the quality signals that determine whether a question will be reused
- Poor-performing questions (low PBI) should be flagged for the faculty's attention

---

## Scope Constraints

- **IN (Phase 1):** Extra time + font size accommodations; live flag monitoring per question; score distribution overview; content area / objective / Bloom's post-exam analysis
- **IN (Phase 1):** Per-question PBI and discrimination coefficient display post-exam
- **DEFERRED:** Speech-to-text (lower priority; check if ExamSoft supports it first)
- **DEFERRED:** Special hardware accommodations (third-party keyboard, mouse)
  > "When we go to speech to text, connecting third party tools and hardware — now is that going to mess up our security?"
- **OUT:** Overall student GPA / academic standing in EM screens
  > "Showing how the student is performing overall or what is GPA — not even that much important in exam management. That should always stay with the student profile."

---

## Data / Entity Rules

- **Accommodation:** Student ↔ Accommodation type (extra_time, font_size, etc.); course-level view of all students with accommodations
- **Question analytics (post-exam):** Per-question: PBI, discrimination coefficient, content area tags, Bloom's level, % correct
- **Flag (live monitoring):** Question order → flag count → acknowledge/dismiss per question
