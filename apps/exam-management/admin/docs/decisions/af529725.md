## User Flows

- **Course coordinator** creates assessment shell → creates sections (one per faculty contributor) → assigns each faculty to their section → faculty adds questions to their section → faculty indicates section is ready → coordinator reviews all sections → coordinator sends to chair for review → chair approves → coordinator publishes / sets download window
  > "I will create the assessment shell, and I will tell Nippon you put it in your section. [...] So now you go to that section and you start putting 10 questions from the question bank that you already have. Or you could write new questions if you want to make edits. But you build those 10 questions, and you indicate that your 10 questions are ready."

- **Faculty (instructor)** receives section access for a course → opens assigned section → picks questions from shared course question bank → adds them to their section → marks as ready
  > "You probably both of you already have access to this course because you are part of the team that teaches this course."

## Design Decisions

- **Review level — assessment only, not section or question:** Assessment-level review is the right unit. Section-level review is not required. Question-level review does not commonly happen.
  > "What I'm saying is that review, we should build first at an assessment level. Then think about building a question level review and all of that because that usually doesn't happen. Assessment level, the review happens."
  > "Section level review to answer your question, Rohit, is not required. Just assessment level review is enough."

- **Review is a differentiator over ExamSoft:** ExamSoft does not have an assessment review/approval workflow. This is a genuine differentiator.
  > "Assessment review is a real thing, which today, ExamSoft does not cater into. It could be a good differentiator for us."

- **Multiple reviewers allowed, typically one chair:** System should not limit to one reviewer, but real usage is always chair or associate dean.
  > "I think they can be multiple, but in most cases, multiple people don't. It's the chair or the supervisor who reviews it. So if there are two — one for sciences, one for clinical — then the respective chairs will review it. So ability to send it for review, we should build that they could potentially send it to two different reviewers. We should not limit."

- **What reviewers evaluate:** Question quality (options, distractors, stem clarity), difficulty mix (easy/medium/hard balance), overall rigor, prior performance statistics if available, Bloom's tags.
  > "They're looking at all the questions, first of all, whether the options provided have good distractors, whether the question stem is ambiguous, is it clear. So the quality of the question writing is an art and a skill. Second, overall, the mix of questions. Is it a good mix easy, medium, and hard?"

- **Tagging during review vs. building:** Tagging (mapping to objectives, competencies, standards) happens during question creation in the question bank — NOT during assessment building or review.
  > "They don't map when they're reviewing. They map when they're building the questions. Viewing the tags is important. Tagging from while they're creating an assessment is not that important."

- **Sections: two distinct use cases:**
  1. Multi-faculty exam: one section per instructor, each names their section (e.g., "Dr. Mehra's Section")
  2. Pre-read: a common case study/text before a group of questions
  > "A very common use case: there are two use cases. One is a multi-team course where there are five faculty that are co-creating this assessment. [...] Second use case for sectioning is the next five to answer the next five question, read this case study."

- **Section access control:** Faculty can SEE questions in other sections but cannot EDIT them. Course coordinator can edit all sections.
  > "Whether Darshan should be able to see the questions that you have added in your section — I would say that at the beginning, it is also safe and also easy for us to allow both Nippon and Darshan to see questions from the other section. But they are not able to edit someone else's question."

- **Section must have free-text title.**
  > "There should be a section title which should be a free customizable text that the coach coordinator can fill in."

## Review & Approval Workflows

- **Trigger:** Course coordinator finishes building assessment → sends to reviewer(s)
- **Reviewers:** Chair (primary), Associate Dean (secondary), multiple allowed
- **Gate type:** SOFT — coordinator can still publish/administer without approval
  > "Even if you are the faculty and your assessment sent it for review and the manager didn't review it. You can still give it to your students. You're not forbidden from giving it to your students."
- **System behavior on unapproved publish:** Show warning "still pending approval" — do not block
  > "If they want to administer it and it has not been approved, you let them administer it. But you say oh, just so you know this is still pending approval."

## Section & Collaboration Rules

- Course coordinator creates the assessment shell and all sections up front
- Coordinator assigns instructors to sections (instructors are already part of the course team)
- Each instructor only contributes to their assigned section
- Instructor marks their section "ready" when done
- Coordinator can prompt instructors with deadlines ("I need 10 questions by this date")
- Questions always stay in the base course question bank — they are LINKED to a section, not copied into it

---

## Scope Constraints

- **IN (Phase 1):** Assessment-level review workflow (send to reviewer, approve, soft gate)
- **IN (Phase 1):** Section creation with free-text title; section-to-instructor assignment
- **IN (Phase 1):** Pre-read material attached to a section
- **DEFERRED:** Question-level review
- **DEFERRED:** Section-level review
- **DEFERRED:** Faculty-to-student associations (not needed for exam management Phase 1)
  > "Faculty associations for advising, I don't see how those associations are needed for exam management."

---

## Data / Entity Rules

- **Student data in EM:** Only exam scores, registration, accommodation info — NOT overall GPA, not full academic standing
  > "From exam management, an administrator or faculty is not going to manage anything else for the student, like their profile or for that matter, not even their academic standing."
- **Course grade vs. exam score:** Exam scores live in EM; final course grades live in LMS (other assessments outside EM factor into course grade)
  > "Course grades are not saved in exam management. They are always saved in LMS."
