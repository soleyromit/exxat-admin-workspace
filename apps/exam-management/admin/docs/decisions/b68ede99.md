## User Flows

- **Faculty opens course → sees assessment overview:** Primary view = list of assessments organized by completion status. Ongoing at top (most urgent), upcoming/scheduled next, completed at bottom.

- **Pop quiz flow:** Faculty is in lecture → opens course in EM → starts new assessment instantly → students in the class see it and begin immediately. No pre-scheduling.
  > "Pop quizzes — I'm in the lecture. Everybody open up your computers, and I'm going to administer this."

---

## Design Decisions

- **Primary org = completion status, NOT workflow status:**
  > "I don't want a lot of it to take up space [workflow status] until we really deploy that feature and we align with the users that this is very helpful. [...] Your primary concern should not be your objectives. When you land from a course, your primary concern should not be assessment approval."
  > "I think completion is a bigger category. So primary concern. Five have been completed. Two are still to be scheduled."

- **Ongoing = most critical state to surface:** An assessment that is currently open and students are taking it is the highest-priority state.
  > "Are the students currently taking it? [...] The assessment is currently ongoing — yes, that is an important thing. Ongoing is important."

- **Three primary states:** Ongoing (open) → Upcoming (scheduled, not yet started) → Completed
  > "Your primary concern is all of your assessments that are scheduled or not yet complete. Your secondary concern is completed assessments."

- **Workflow / approval status = secondary widget only:**
  > "You can create a widget that says assessment approval status or whatever. But it needs to be a lower priority thing."

- **Summary stats on overview:** Could show e.g. "7 assessments, 5 completed, 2 scheduled" — but completion-based grouping, not workflow-based.
  > "Seven assessments, five completed, two scheduled or whatever. You can either decide completion is the main category."

- **Pop quiz = "start now" flow:** Faculty can start an assessment immediately during a live class. The concept of a lecture/event triggering an assessment is separate from scheduled assessments.
  > "Pop quiz — is it possible that an assessment is open, but like, from my point of view, my assessments could be open for thirty minutes or twenty minutes in my class. If it's ongoing, it becomes critical."

- **Assessment review soft gate — do not block publishing:**
  > "Even if you are the faculty and your assessment sent it for review and your manager didn't review it. You can still give it to your students. You're not forbidden from giving it to your students. Only for certain assessment types may they want sign-off — big exams, finals. But we should not mandate it."
  > "We should introduce it as a silent feature. If they want to administer it and it has not been approved, you let them administer it. But you say, oh, just so you know this is still pending approval."

---

## Review & Approval Workflows

- Assessment review is a SECONDARY concern — it should appear as a widget/status chip, not as the primary organizer of the assessment list
- For big exams (finals, summative) some programs may want approval before publishing — but the system should NOT enforce this as a hard gate
- Status: "Pending review" / "Approved" shown as a badge on the assessment card, not as a section heading

---

## Scope Constraints

- **IN (Phase 1):** Completion-based organization (Ongoing, Upcoming, Completed)
- **IN (Phase 1):** Pop quiz / instant-start flow
- **IN (Phase 1):** Approval status as a secondary widget (soft)
- **DEFERRED:** Live proctoring
  > "We are not doing live proctoring today."
- **OUT:** Organizing assessments by workflow/approval status as the PRIMARY dimension

---

## Data / Entity Rules

- Assessment states: `ongoing` (open, students taking) > `scheduled` (set start time, not yet open) > `draft` (being built) > `completed` (closed)
- Pop quiz state: can transition directly from `draft` to `ongoing` without a `scheduled` phase
